Learn Onboarding

Addition of a progress widget to optimize the onboarding track for Flatiron School’s online learning platform, Learn.co

Problem

Users were dropping off during the onboarding process, especially around the download and installation of a tool called the IDE. A tool that is essential for the platform’s workflow for solving labs.

My Role

I worked with a product manager and our QA engineer to identify potential problem spots in the onboarding track, brainstorm solutions and then A/B test our proposed solution against the current track.

Identifying Potential Pain Points

We set a meeting to review the entire onboarding track. Our QA engineer was able to identify specific points in the process where users were dropping off. We hypothesized some users might be getting discouraged not knowing how many steps they had left complete or how long it would take them to get set up before they can actually start their course.

Pain Point

Users had no way of knowing how many steps they needed to complete to finish or how long it would take them to complete the onboarding track

Adding Progress Indication

I started sketching out some ideas in high fidelity by modifying the existing UI for our lesson page. We already had some indication for progress for each lesson in the right rail. I tried some iterations that made the right rail persistent and added an additional indication of progress at the track level.

A persistent right rail with progress at both lesson and track level wasn’t working. It was overcomplicated and less clear when a student completed all tasks for a specific lesson and were ready to move to the next.

The Next Iteration

In the next iteration I added a toggle to the right rail that enable students to flip the right rail up to see how there progress through the whole track and then flip it back down to complete the lesson.

Final Solution

The final solution was a widget that sat on top of the existing right rail. It can be toggled open to reveal all steps in the track. When closed a progress bar still gives an indication of where they are in the track.
